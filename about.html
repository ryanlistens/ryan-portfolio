About

I'm a writer who ended up in tech, then nonprofits, then back to tech—always translating complexity into something human.

I've documented supply chain systems for Microsoft, written donor campaigns for waterfront advocacy groups, and crafted DEI content for staffing firms. The through-line: taking messy, important work and making it clear to whoever needs to understand it.

Originally from the Philly area, got degrees in journalism and English, spent years in Boston doing communications work, and now I'm in Connecticut looking to get back into environmental and mission-driven spaces.

Outside of work: I write fiction about industrial decay and collective action, do stand-up comedy, make music, and think a lot about systems—how they break, how they're built, how they could be better.
